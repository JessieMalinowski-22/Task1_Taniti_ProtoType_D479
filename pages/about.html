Welcome to Taniti Island

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.

Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Visitor Essentials

  • Power outlets are 120 volts (same as U.S.).
  • Alcohol is not sold from midnight to 9:00 a.m.
  • Drinking age is 18 but not strictly enforced.
  • English is widely spoken in Taniti City, less so in rural areas.
  • There is one hospital and several clinics, with multilingual staff.
  • Violent crime is rare, though petty theft has risen with tourism.
  • U.S. dollar is used; many places also accept euros and yen.
  • Check ahead for holiday closures at restaurants and attractions.

Currency & Communication

The U.S. dollar is the official currency. Credit cards are accepted widely, and currency exchange is available at major banks.
